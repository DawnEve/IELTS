entail /ɪnˈteɪl/  CET6 TEM8 ( entailing, entailed, entails )
V-T If one thing entails another, it involves it or causes it. 牵连; 导致 [正式]

Such a decision would entail a huge political risk in the midst of the presidential campaign.
这样的决定会在总统大选之际导致一个巨大的政治风险。

It will entail driving a long distance every day. 
这意味着每天都要长途开车。

(2) 剑桥词典
to make something necessary, or to involve something 使必要；牵涉

Such a large investment inevitably entails some risk.
这样的巨额投资必然会有一定的风险。

[ + -ing verb ] Repairing the roof will entail spending a lot of money.
修房顶免不了要花好多钱。

(3) 
However, this withdrawal of scholars did not entail the disappearance of the word from popular language, quite the contrary. [nature]
然而，学者们的撤离并不意味着这个词从通俗语言中消失，恰恰相反。
